Rice EMS operates as a branch of the Rice University Police
Department. The RUPD Chief also serves as the Director of Public
Safety for Rice University.

Chief Whitehead is a graduate of the FBI National Academy,
the Federal Executive Institute and the Harvard University
Kennedy School of Government Crisis Leadership in Higher
Education Program. He has a bachelor's degree in political
science from the University of Baltimore and a master's in
applied behavioral science from Johns Hopkins University.
Our Medical Directors are Emergency Physicians committed to
excellence in Emergency Medicine and EMS. Some of them are
alumni of Rice University and Rice EMS. They are available
around the clock to provide emergency medical direction
for the On-Duty Campus Crew.
The EMS Director is responsible for supporting REMS
administration needs (budgeting, special projects, etc.)
and ensures long-term projects are completed.

The Rice EMS Captain is the highest-ranking student in Rice
EMS. The Captain is responsible for all patient care and
operational aspects of the department, overseeing the works
of the Lieutenants.
The Senior Lieutenant is the second-in-command of Rice EMS.
The Senior Lieutenant assists the captain in overseeing all
aspects of patient care, operations, and education offered
by Rice EMS.
Several alumni of Rice EMS, physicians in the Texas Medical
Center, and EMS providers in Houston area contribute their time
and expertise to help Rice EMS Education and Special Event
programs.
The InCharge is an operational rank referring to the EMT who is
in command of a scene. The On-Duty InCharge responds in the
marked Rice EMS vehicle that carries advanced medical equipment.
The InCharge approves all patient care decisions during his/her
shift. The InCharge is an EMT-Intermediate with Advanced Life
Support training.
The rank of Lieutenant is given to members of the department
who are responsible to managing different administrative areas
of the organization, and have additional responsibilities which
include everything from scheduling special events coverage to
running the EMS Education Program.
Duty Crew members are the backbone of Rice EMS, and include both
EMT-Basics (BLS - Basic Life Support training) and
EMT-Intermediates (ALS - Advanced Life Support training).
Duty Crew members respond to campus emergencies along with the
InCharges. Duty Crew members can also be cleared to work Special
Events (athletic events, Baker Institute events, public parties,
etc.) and become BLS Instructors (teach CPR/First Aid classes).
