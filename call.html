Never go offline until the dispatcher does.
The "fourth floor of Fondren Library near call number DA89"
is more helpful than "Fondren Library." It is also a good idea
to have someone waiting outside the building to help direct
Rice EMS to the scene.
Indicate the severity of the problem. A hand bleeding from a
paper cut is a different scenario than a hand bleeding from a
gunshot wound. If the emergency is medical (nausea, chest pain,
dizziness), it may be helpful to give the age of the patient.
If there is a threat to your safety, (fire, violent person,
chemical spill, etc.) leave immediately. If you can safely
help remove the injured person from the scene, do so, but
your safety comes first. It is vital that you tell dispatch
if there is a safety threat, otherwise EMTs may be harmed.
Obviously, if the EMTs are injured, they can no longer
help the patient.
Rice EMTs are well-trained, and have a lot of experience
helping injured and sick people. They have literally saved
lives on numerous occasions, and are more than ready to
take on any situation.
If you or someone else on the scene has first aid training,
try to help the patient. Never attempt to do anything that
you have not been trained to do, or feel uncomfortable doing.
Generally, 1 Duty Crew EMT and 1 EMS InCharge will
respond; RUPD may also be dispatched, depending on
the nature of the emergency.
The InCharge may request additional assistance as needed.
For our patients' safety and well-being, Rice EMS
always recommends that patients seek further medical
attention at a hospital using ambulance transport.
REMS always recommends ambulance transport, as that
is the safest option for our patients; for minor injuries,
(twisted ankle, minor laceration, etc.) the patient may
be taken to the hospital by a friend.
More serious cases (or patient's request) will require
ambulance transport to the hospital. Dispatch will
contact HFD directly. RUPD will guide HFD units to
the appropriate location. REMS will continue to the
treat the patient and transfer patient care upon HFD's arrival.

REMS and RUPD offer their services free of charge, but
Rice has no control over costs incurred by HFD transport;
patients transported by HFD should expected to be billed
for that service (insurance should assist in the payment).
If you cannot pay the fee and your insurance does not cover
ambulance transport, HFD has a no-interest payment plan available.
Any competent patient has the right to make his or
her own medical decisions, including refusing further
treatment (after an assessment of the patient's
competency has been performed).
